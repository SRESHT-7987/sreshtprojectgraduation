Iconic Space Explorers
1. Sally Ride : In 1983, Sally Ride became the first LGBTQ astronaut and the first American woman to travel to space. She was responsible for operating the robotic arm in the Challenger .
2.Yuri Gagarin : Russian cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin became the first man to travel to space in 1961. The space flight, during which Gagarin spent 108 minutes in space, took off from this site in Kazakhstan.
3. Neil Armstrong : He became the first man to walk on the moon in 1969. Along with Buzz Aldrin, Armstrong spent nearly 22 hours on the moon during the Apollo 11 mission. The Armstrong Air & Space Museum, shaped like a moon base, is located in his hometown of Wapakoneta, Ohio.
4. Kirobo : Launched from JAXA’s Tanegashima Space Center in 2013, the 34-centimeter-tall Kirobo is the first robot astronaut in space. As a companion robot, Kirobo was programmed to recognize its commander’s face and voice in order to provide emotional support.

6. Carl Sagan : He visited this planetarium, and the rest, as they say, is history. The author of 600 scientific papers was widely known for his 1980 TV series, "Cosmos: A Personal Voyage," which was watched by more than 500 million people globally. Sagan helped make science accessible to everyone.
7. Stephen Hawking : Regarded as one of the greatest scientific minds, Stephen Hawking produced groundbreaking work on black holes and the laws of the universe. Hawking both studied and taught at the University of Cambridge. In 1963.
8. Mae Jemison : He is not only the first African-American woman to travel to space, she is also the first real-life astronaut to guest star in an episode of "Star Trek: Next Generation." Following her departure from NASA, she founded her own company and continues to advocate for science .
9. Anuradha TK : As Program Director of the Indian Space Research Organization, Anuradha TK alongside other women scientists helped launching the Mangalyaan or the Mars Orbiter Mission, which is gathering information about the surface and atmosphere of Mars.
10. Peggy Whitson : She served as the first female commander of the International Space Station. She also holds several records including the most spacewalks by a female astronaut and the the most time spent in space — 665 days in total .
