Big Idea 3

"Changing the movement of an object requires a net force to be acting on it."
 Key Points:
 Movement/Motion : The action done by any body. Whenever any body moves from one position to another with time, it is called Movement/Motion of the body. Force is applied to start/stop any object to move.
Newton's Laws of Motion: First Law : An object at rest tends to stay at rest, and an object in motion tends to stay in motion with the same direction and speed, until, an external force is applied on it.
Newton's Laws of Motion: Second Law : The acceleration of an object produced by an applied force is directly related to the magnitude of the force, the same direction as the force and inversely related to the mass of the object

Big Idea 7

Organisms are organised on a cellullar basis
    It is all about cells, tissues, organs, organ system and Organisms
    These all things will form an organism but what are these ? Well, this is the hierarchy of life let me explain this in detail
  • Cells are tiny and most important things which make every thing in our body ex blood cells
  • Tissues are a group of cells that have similar structure and that function together as a unit. Ex blood tissues
  • Organ is a collection of tissues that structurally form a functional unit specialized to perform a particular function.
  • Organ system is a biological system consisting of a group of organs that work together to perform one or more functions
  • Organism is a set of organ systems

Big Idea 4

"The total amount of energy in the universe is always the same. But energy can be transformed when things change or are made to happen."
 Key Points:
 Energy : Ability to do work, make things happen and to cause changes. It can neither be created nor destroyed
Classification of Energy : Forms of energy based on movement:
Potential Energy : The amount of energy which is contained in the object
Kinetic energy : The amount of energy of an object in motion
